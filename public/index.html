Step 1: What are the Greeks?

The "Greeks" are risk sensitivities that measure how an option's price changes with respect to various factors. They're called Greeks because most are represented by Greek letters (Œî, Œì, Œò, ŒΩ, œÅ).

Think of Greeks as:
  • ‚Ä¢ Delta (Œî): Speed - how fast price moves with stock
  • ‚Ä¢ Gamma (Œì): Acceleration - how speed changes
  • ‚Ä¢ Theta (Œò): Time decay - daily price erosion
  • ‚Ä¢ Vega (ŒΩ): Volatility sensitivity
  • ‚Ä¢ Rho (œÅ): Interest rate sensitivity

üéØ Options Basics - Interactive Tutorial

Tutorial Progress Step 1 of 6
Step 1: What is an Option?

An option is a contract that gives you the RIGHT (not obligation) to buy or sell a stock at a specific price by a certain date. Think of it like a reservation at a restaurant - you have the right to use it, but you don't have to.

Key Components:
  • ‚Ä¢ Strike Price: The price at which you can buy/sell
  • ‚Ä¢ Expiration Date: When the option expires
  • ‚Ä¢ Premium: The cost to buy the option
  • ‚Ä¢ Underlying Asset: The stock the option is based on

Understanding the Greeks

‚ö†Ô∏è

Common Confusion: Rho ‚â† Risk-Free Rate

The risk-free rate is the actual interest rate (like 4%). Rho is how much your option price changes when that rate moves by 1%.

Delta (Œî)

Speed of price change

If stock moves $1, option moves ~$Delta. Call: 0-1, Put: -1-0

Gamma (Œì)

Acceleration

How much Delta changes per $1 stock move. Higher = more responsive

Theta (Œò)

Time decay

$ lost per day. Always negative - time kills options

Vega (V)

Volatility sensitivity

$ change per 1% volatility move (25% ‚Üí 26%)

Rho (œÅ)

Rate sensitivity

$ change per 1% rate move (4% ‚Üí 5%). Call: +, Put: -

Real-World Example

üì± AAPL $150 Call, $155 Strike, 30 days
Delta = 0.35: If AAPL rises $1 ‚Üí Call gains ~$0.35
Gamma = 0.02: That 0.35 delta becomes ~0.37
Theta = -0.08: Loses $0.08 every day that passes
Vega = 0.12: If volatility rises 1% ‚Üí Call gains $0.12
Rho = 0.27: If rates rise from 4%‚Üí5% ‚Üí Call gains $0.27
üéØ Key Insights
  • ‚Ä¢ All Greeks are rates of change (derivatives)
  • ‚Ä¢ Delta shows directional exposure
  • ‚Ä¢ Gamma shows how fast that changes
  • ‚Ä¢ Theta is your daily bleed
  • ‚Ä¢ Vega matters most for earnings/events
  • ‚Ä¢ Rho barely matters unless rates are moving fast
